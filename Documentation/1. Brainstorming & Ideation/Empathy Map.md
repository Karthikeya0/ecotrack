# Empathy Map
## EcoTrack – AI-Powered Carbon Footprint Tracker

---

## Overview

An Empathy Map is a collaborative visualization used to articulate what we know about a particular type of user. It externalizes knowledge about users in order to create a shared understanding and aid in decision making. This empathy map was developed for EcoTrack's primary target user.

---

## Primary User: "The Environmentally Conscious Urban Professional"

**Profile:**
- Age: 22–38
- Location: Urban / suburban area
- Education: University-educated
- Income: Lower-middle to upper-middle class
- Tech comfort: High – uses smartphone daily, comfortable with web apps
- Environmental interest: Moderate-to-high – cares about climate, makes some eco-conscious choices

---

## Empathy Map Canvas

### 👁️ SEES
- News and social media coverage of climate change, wildfires, and extreme weather
- Friends and colleagues making eco-conscious choices (EV cars, veganism, cycling)
- Advertisements for carbon-neutral products and sustainability pledges by brands
- Charts showing rising CO₂ levels and shrinking Arctic ice
- Fellow commuters stuck in traffic, generating idle emissions
- Price increases on energy bills and fuel, linked to environmental regulations

### 👂 HEARS
- "We all need to do our part" from media, celebrities, and activists
- Conflicting information: "Individual actions don't matter vs. collective change starts with individuals"
- Friends sharing sustainable swaps and lifestyle changes
- Headlines about net-zero targets from governments and corporations
- "The planet is warming too fast" from climate scientists
- Advice from apps and newsletters that feels too generic to act on

### 💬 SAYS
- "I want to be more eco-friendly but I don't know where to start"
- "I recycle and take shorter showers — I do my bit"
- "I don't want to give up flying / meat completely"
- "I wish I knew what actually makes a difference vs. what's just greenwashing"
- "I'd love an app that tells me how to reduce my footprint specifically"
- "I don't have time for complicated tools — it needs to be quick"

### 🧠 THINKS
- "Am I doing enough? My individual impact feels too small to matter"
- "I want to track this but it seems like a lot of effort"
- "I wonder how much CO₂ my commute actually produces"
- "What changes would make the biggest difference to MY footprint?"
- "I'd be more motivated if I could see real progress over time"
- "I don't want to create yet another account just to try an app"

## Pain Points

| Pain Point | Severity | EcoTrack Response |
|---|---|---|
| Information overload — too many generic tips | High | AI generates personalized advice from user's own log |
| Effort required to track daily habits | High | 3-step wizard, <2 min to complete |
| No visible progress or reward | High | Streak tracking + badge achievement system |
| Account friction blocks first experience | Medium | Demo Mode — instant access, no login |
| Distrust of approximate emission estimates | Medium | Carbon Interface API + transparent factors |
| Forgetting to log consistently | Medium | Dashboard streak reminder, visual gap indicator |

---

## Gains & Motivators

| Gain | How EcoTrack Delivers It |
|---|---|
| Clarity about personal impact | Emissions gauge with daily budget comparison |
| Sense of progress and achievement | Streaks, badges, historical trend charts |
| Actionable, relevant advice | Groq AI generates tips from the user's specific log data |
| Convenience and speed | Demo Mode, quick 3-step wizard |
| Trust in data accuracy | Carbon Interface API, transparent local calculation logic |
| Habit formation | Daily streak reward, visual consistency tracker |

## Emotional Journey Through EcoTrack

